Biography

Jan Snakowski was born in Poland in 1990. He studied at his native Academy of Music in Cracow, from which he graduated with honors in violin and viola. Later on he continued his studies at the Haute Ecole de Musique in Lausanne, Switzerland with Prof. Alexander Zemtsov.
He is a laureate of international solo- and chamber music competitions, most recently the 1st Prize at the 21st International Johannes Brahms Competition in Poertschach, Austria. Since then, Jan has performed several times as a soloist with orchestras in Poland and abroad, aside from regular activity as a chamber musician.
Jan also possesses exceptionally broad experience as an orchestral musician. Currently, he is the deputy Solo Viola of Sinfonieorchester St. Gallen in Switzerland. Before that he was a member of the 1st Violin section of the Cracow Philharmonic and guest player in orchestras such as Sinfonia Varsovia, Sinfonieorchester Basel, Orchestre de Chambre de Lausanne, led by world-class conductors including Lorin Maazel, Jukka-Pekka Saraste, Dennis Russel Davies and many others.
Jan is a scholarship holder of the polish Sapere Auso foundation and alumnus of the Verbier Festival Academy. In 2016 he was a jury member of the International Johannes Brahms Competition.
Jan plays on a 1981 Marius Cappichioni viola, a generous loan from Mr. Paul Westermayer.

Repertoire

Jan's repertoire consists of most of the greatest works of the standard viola repertoire, such as the viola concertos by Bartok, Walton, Hindemith and Forsyth, Mozart's Sinfonia Concertante, as well as sonatas for viola and piano and other chamber music pieces by Brahms, Schumann, Schubert and others. He also performs works from his homeland and original transcriptions.
